Instead we propose that our noun describes an instrument used for night-fishing. In the vast study Ancient Fishing Gear and Associated Artifacts from Underwater Explorations in Israel — A Comparative Study (Haifa, 2013) the authors Galili, Zemer and Rosen include a survey of the practice of fire-fishing, and mention an "iron fire basket for maintaining fire, probably a light-emitting device used to lure marine creatures" (see 2 Corinthians 11:29). They furthermore attest that "Fishing by fire was practiced since prehistoric times". The whole idea of night-fishing was to dazzle fish, marine mammals and octopuses with a great and luring but synthetic light in the middle of the natural night. Particularly those zealous fish who had studied Isaiah 9:2 but not the mechanism of the solar system or the predictability of natural cycles, would rise in jubilation only to be met by the sharp end of a pronged harpoon.
